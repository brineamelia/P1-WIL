Impact

Greater profitability Time and again, evidence supports the theory that gender diversity has a positive impact on the bottom line. According to McKinsey, the most gender-diverse companies are 21% more like to experience above-average profitability.

Company 1 https://www.thetimes.co.uk/static/tt50/ ACCENTURE We are a leading global professional services company providing a broad range of services in strategy and consulting, interactive, technology and operations, with digital capabilities across all of the above. Our goal is to achieve a gender-balanced workforce by 2025, and we have hit the following interim goals: almost 60 per cent of new hires are women; we’ve promoted our largest percentage of women to managing director level; we’ve grown our percentage of female managing directors.

Company 2 EY We are one of the world’s largest professional services networks and accountancy firms, comprising four service lines: assurance; consulting; strategy and transactions; and tax. Diversity and inclusion (D&I) is the firm’s number one strategic priority, and our D&I Strategy puts gender equality at its heart, through a “differential focus on race and gender”. We publicly commit to 40 per cent female representation in our partnership by 2025 and we have doubled our investment in coaching and sponsorship programmes for women. https://www.thetimes.co.uk/static/tt50/

Company 3 NATWEST GROUP We are a UK-headquartered company, employing around 62,000 staff globally, and supporting more than 19 million customers to access international markets in Europe, Asia, the Middle East and North America. We’re proud that, in addition to improving the proportion of women in our pipeline by 11 per cent (to 43 per cent) and in our most senior roles by 8 per cent (to 37 per cent), our workforce engagement scores are the highest they have ever been since we started measuring colleague sentiment 10 years ago. https://www.thetimes.co.uk/static/tt50/
