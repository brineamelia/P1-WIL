Leaders

Maggie Timoney

Timoney is the first woman to serve as chief executive of a major beer company in the United States. “I did not get this job because I am a woman. I got this job because I have left previous companies in a better place for having been there and that is what my job is today. I am a CEO that happens to be a woman, not a CEO because I am a woman.”

Safra Catz

Safra Catz has served as CEO of software firm Oracle since September 2014. Catz was born in Israel, earned a law degree at University of Pennsylvania and worked on Wall Street for 14 years covering the software industry. When asked what she thinks is the most significant barrier to female leadership? Safra Catz responded: "The most significant barrier to female leadership is the actual lack of females in leadership. The best advice I can give to women is to go out and start something, ideally their own businesses. If you can't see a path for leadership within your own company, go blaze a trail of your own."

Whitney Wolfe-Herd

Whitney Wolfe-Herd is not only one of very few female billionaires, but is now the youngest woman to take a company public in the US  Whitney launched Bumble, a female-focused app dedicated to women making the first move. Within the first year Bumble has more than 15 million conversations and 8million matches.

Whitney Wolfe-Herd: "To all the first movers, whether on our platforms, in business, or in life: you’re what today is about. Never take no for an answer, believe in yourselves, and turn your pain into purpose."
